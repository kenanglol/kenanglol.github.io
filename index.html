About

I am a person who loves lifelong learning and thinking about the future. I think progression and self-development can only be possible with innovation and expertise. I enjoy my job and trying to integrate it into my life. I remember CPU Scheduling algorithms when I prepared my daily schedule. The shortest path algorithm is coming to my mind when I am traveling. I think that it is very difficult to separate our lives from our profession, and professional wisdom is very important in life.
I am enthusiastic and curious about many areas of computer science. But my favorite topic to ponder is software architecture. When developing software, I attach great importance to designing with all the requirements in mind and discovering brilliant ideas by brainstorming. I have been developing a microservice architecture for about a year at my workplace. Truly a relaxing and happy experience for me. The torture of fixing up huge apps run locally is gone, and the unknown side effects of new developments are less than in monolith projects. I have been developing myself recently in areas such as Unit Testing and Spring Boot to experience more of this architecture, where I can fully focus on software development.
